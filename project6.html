Assistive Guitar Strumming Device

Engineering Design Engineering Drawing Machine Shop Technical Documentation

The goal is to redesign the device to alleviate biomechanical strain, enabling individuals with limited upper limb mobility to engage in guitar playing more comfortably. The proposed device aims to seamlessly integrate with CanAssist's existing strumming mechanism while prioritizing portability, versatility across different user profiles, and affordability.

Key Features

Heel-strumming mechanism

Allows for stronger biomechanical action compared to original toe-strumming design

Strumming Ability

Can pull the pick 4 cm across the strings

Low-Cost

The design is laser cut from plywood making it <$15 to produce

Technical Challenges

The technical challenges we faced were creating a strong enough device to pull the device across the guitar strings with the time and money constraints. Given more time and money, changing the material from laser-cut plywood to steel would allow the device to withstand the forces necessary to strum the guitar.
